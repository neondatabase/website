## Spending alerts

Organization admins can now get **spending alerts** when monthly charges approach or reach a [spending limit](/docs/introduction/spending-limit). Neon emails admins at **80%** and **100%** of the limit, checks spending about every **15 minutes**, and sends weekly reminders until you raise the limit or the billing cycle resets. You set and manage limits on the **Billing** page in the Neon Console (Launch and Scale plans).

Today, alerts are the primary action when a limit is configured. [Automatic project suspension](/docs/introduction/spending-limit#what-happens-when-the-limit-is-reached) when the limit is reached is coming soon. For full behavior, who can change limits, and what happens at the limit, see **[Spending limit](/docs/introduction/spending-limit)**.

## Durable AI agents with Pydantic AI, DBOS, and Neon

Multi-step agents depend on LLMs and tools that can time out, rate limit, or fail mid-run. A practical pattern is **durable execution**: checkpoint progress in **Postgres** so a workflow can resume after a crash or retry without redoing expensive steps from scratch. **DBOS** provides that durable execution layer, **Pydantic AI** structures agent logic and tool orchestration, and **Neon** backs the database so checkpoint state lives in serverless Postgres that scales with your workload.

For a full walkthrough with a working example, see [**Building Durable AI Agents with Pydantic AI, DBOS, and Neon**](/guides/pydantic-ai-dbos-neon).
